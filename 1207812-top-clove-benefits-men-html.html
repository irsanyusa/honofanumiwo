Unusual-looking clove buds are famous. Their popularity stems from their burning taste, spicy aroma, and healing effects. The spice has long been used in traditional medicine recipes for the prevention and even treatment of certain diseases.

What are cloves?

They are spice made from the flower buds of an evergreen clove tree (Syzygium aromaticum). The flower buds are harvested in their immature state and then dried. Whole cloves are shaped like a small, reddish-brown spike, usually around a centimeter in length, with a bulbous top. They can be used whole or ground, and they have a powerful, pungent flavour and aroma.
