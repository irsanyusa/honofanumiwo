LEGO Masters Season 3 will return to FOX on Wednesday night, airing a two-hour finale as the winning team is revealed. The show is taking over The Masked Singer‘s previous timeslot now that the show has finished for the season.

LEGO Masters Season 3 to Air Two-Hour Finale

The end of the season will air in two parts on Wednesday night. In the first hour, the Semifinalists will be challenged to build a fountain. Three teams will then move on to the finale, where they’ll have 24 hours to complete their final builds.

It all starts on Wednesday night at 8/7c on FOX. At the end of the night, a winning team will be announced. They’ll receive the $100,000 grand prize and the LEGO Masters trophy.

As we head into the final night of the competition, four teams remain — brothers Brendan & Greg, siblings Dave & Emily, influencers Nick & Stacey, and firefighters Stephen & Stephen. Only three of them can move on to the final challenge, and only one will win.

The Show’s Fans Have Had to Keep Up with Changes

This season of LEGO Masters featured quite a few schedule shake-ups that fans had to keep track of. To kick things off, the season was originally supposed to premiere back in May, before it was pushed back until September.

Then later in the season, the show was preempted by baseball, along with its lead-in The Masked Singer. The schedule was also changed from Wednesday to Sunday for one week, and then two episodes aired the week of Thanksgiving, including one on Thanksgiving day.
